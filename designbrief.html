Go green. Make the switch today.

ESA stands for Environmentally Friendly Automobiles. Our mission is to be at the forefront of sustainable automobile research and manufacturing. We’re leading advancements in the energy efficiency and affordability of electric vehicles in order to bring them to a mass market in hopes of making a faster transition from fossil fuels to sustainable electric alternatives.





Image by LEEROY Agency from Pixabay

Did you know that your current car alone can emit almost 12,000 lbs of carbon dioxide per year*? Take that number, and multiply it by the millions of cars on the road. It all adds up to billions of lbs in CO2 emissions each year. We encourage people to take initiative to be more mindful about energy-saving measures that add up to a world of difference in lowering our greenhouse gas emissions.


*Calculation made assuming that the average car gets 20 miles per gallon with a typical amount of mileage per year.

Image by Pixaline from Pixabay

Other than the environmental benefits of electric vehicles, there are a plethora of other advantages offered by replacing your current car with one. Induction motors found in electric vehicles have a much flatter torque-speed curve compared to the internal combustion engine. This allows for much higher low-end torque, enabling faster acceleration times. Electric vehicles also utilize variable inverters to control the angular frequency of the alternating current the supply to the induction motor to vary the torque-speed curve, allowing for much higher efficiency compared to ICE (above 90% from battery to wheel is possible). Because of the flat curve and variable inverter, Electric vehicles are able to function without the use of a complex multi-speed transmission, dramatically increasing manufacturing and maintenance simplicity. Electric vehicles utilizing multiple induction motors for independently powered wheels can also have independent speed control for different wheels to improve turning. They use a large lithium ion battery pack located on the bottom of the vehicle, significantly reducing the height of the center of gravity. A lower center of gravity increases stability and makes it harder to roll over, increasing the vehicle's safety. Furthermore, electric vehicles also commonly implement regenerative braking – part of the energy used to accelerate the car when braking is recovered, resulting in a higher efficiency.


All of our vehicles are powered by electricity rather than gasoline or diesel. We're fully committed to the electric future, and we're taking large steps to get there quicker. Over the next 10 years, we plan on partnering with existing gas stations on a joint venture to allow electric charging in already-established refueling locations. Current technology allows electric vehicles to be charged in as few as 15 minutes through 175kW and 350kW DC Fast Charging technology, adding 200/400 kilometers of car range, respectively. We're working on solutions that could reduce this time even further so that more people can charge their cars more conveniently. By both increasing the availability and speed of electric vehicle refueling stations, we hope to accelerate the transition toward a more eco-friendly future.
