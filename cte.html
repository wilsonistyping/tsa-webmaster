The Engineering Magnet Program

Our engineering magnet program offers a hands-on approach to the engineering process that gives students plentiful experience with professional engineering equipment and software. Students take five courses in the PLTW engineering track, along with electives that allow engineering students to receive a specialized education in topics they are passionate about. Students have the opportunity to earn AutoDesk CAD, Inventor, and Revit certifications throughout their four years in the engineering program.

Furthermore, Middleton High School has a special partnership with the University of South Florida's Electrical Engineering department which allows students to gain a head start in college-level engineering classes. Middleton and USF are looking to expand this program next year to include more electrical engineering classes, including Embedded Systems and Microprocessors.

Our engineering program is supported by four passionate teachers:
Mr. Dodson, Mrs. Genera, Mr. Mead, and Mr. Grieshop.

Typical Engineering Progession

Intro to Engineering Design [9th]


Students learn the basics of modeling and product design through Inventor.

Taught by Mrs. Gerena.

Drafting I [9th]


Students learn AutoCAD and pick up drafting fundamentals in this introductory drafting course. Students can elect to take higher level drafting courses past 9th grade.

Taught by Mr. Dodson.

Computer Integrated Manufacturing [10th]


Students explore various manufacturing processes, the history of manufacturing, and are trained to use CNC (Computer Numeric Control) machines to manufacture their own products.

Taught by Mr. Mead.

Digital Electronics [10th]


Students learn the basics of analog and digital circuit theory and practice, along with breadboard design and the use of Multisim software to test out their circuits.

Taught by Mr. Grieshop.

Principles of Engineering [11th]


Students apply math and physics to explore engineering concepts like simple machines, fluid power, electricity, thermodynamics, and more.

Taught by Mr. Mead.

Aerospace Engineering [Elective]


Students learn about aircraft, navigation, and various concepts related to flight including flight physics and physiology. Students use the Systems Toolkit 11 modeling software to simulate aircraft/spacecraft trajectories.

Taught by Mr. Mead.

Civil Engineering [Elective]


Students learn about the history of building design and explore structural concepts. Students use Revit to design and model structures.

Taught by Mrs. Gerena.

EEL2161 - C Programming [Elective]


A USF dual enrollment partnership class where students learn to use C to create, simulate, and analyze engineering solutions related to electrical engineering.

EEL3705 - Digital Circuits [Elective]


A USF dual enrollment partnership class that introduces intermediate circuit theory concepts with physical circuit design projects, including counters, binary adders, and shift registers.

Engineering Design & Development [12th]


A capstone course for students in the engineering magnet program to design and manufacture a product from start to finish. Students present their final product at the end of the year.

Taught by Mrs. Gerena
